Black Mold Remediation & Testing in Ross, CA

Many home- and business owners associate mold growth with water leaks and property damage. While mold can certainly wreak havoc on a building, its effects on human heath are even more disconcerting.

This is especially true regarding black mold (Stachybotrys). A black mold outbreak can release mycotoxins into the air, which can travel through the HVAC system.

Mycotoxins can adversely affect a person’s neurological, cardiovascular, respiratory and reproductive systems. Prolonged exposure can cause coma or even death.

If you see a black mold outbreak, get everyone out of the building and schedule a mold inspection immediately. Mycotoxins are particularly harmful to people with weak immune systems, such as the elderly and infants, but they can cause serious health complications for healthy adults.

Do You Have a Black Mold Outbreak?


Black mold gets its name from its greenish-black appearance. If you see a black mold outbreak, keep your distance. Do not look for other active sites.

Unlike other mold species, black mold requires at least one week of undisturbed moisture to colonize. As a result, it often grows in areas that are difficult to see, such as basements, behind walls or on top of roofs.

Many business- and homeowners do not suspect black mold until somebody complains about persistent allergy symptoms. Red or itchy eyes, a swollen throat, coughing and dizziness can indicate mycotoxin poisoning.

Mold also has a musty odor. Do not mistake this smell as a typical “basement” scent. If you notice an odd smell, or if you or a family member suffers from persistent or unseasonal allergies, schedule a mold inspection immediately. This is especially important if you suffered a pipe leak or water damage in the last 30 days.

Black Mold Can Cause Potentially Fatal Allergic Reactions


When most people think of allergies, they imagine coughing, sneezing or skin irritations. Reactions to black mold, however, are far more serious.

Initial exposure to mycotoxins may cause dizziness and disorientation, but over time, these symptoms will worsen. Prolonged exposure can lead to:


  • Nose bleeds;

  • Heartbeat irregularities;

  • Inflammation of the heart;

  • Fatigue;

  • Cognitive disorders;

  • Anxiety;

  • And headaches.


If left unaddressed, exposure to mycotoxins can cause coma or even death. It can also cause fetal development issues for pregnant women.

Unfortunately, some of the health effects of black mold are permanent. In most cases, though, the symptoms tend to subside after the mold has been removed.

What Are Trichothecene Mycotoxins?


There are dozens of different mycotoxins, but trichothecene mycotoxins are among the most lethal. In fact, trichothecene mycotoxins have been used in biological weapons. They are also difficult to kill.

Not all black mold outbreaks release trichothecene mycotoxins, but if you notice a black mold growth, you should assume the worst and get everyone out of the building. Schedule a black mold test as soon as possible.

Checking House for Mold


Because black mold requires an undisturbed area, it often grows in places that are difficult to notice. The most common areas are behind walls and in basements. If you recently suffered water damage, you should schedule an immediate mold inspection.

In nature, black mold grows on soil and plant debris. Its biological composition allows it to thrive on construction materials. As a result, black mold can wreak havoc on homes and businesses.
