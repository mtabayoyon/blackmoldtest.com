Common Causes of Black Mold Growth


Stachybotrys chartarum grows naturally in many countries throughout the world. It is particularly common in warm, moist environments. It thrives on soil and plant debris, but it can also grow on most construction materials.

If an area is exposed to moisture for a prolonged period of time, which is common if a pipe leaks, then black mold growth is a real concern. Airborne spores can spread throughout the air ducts and HVAC system, which can lead to outbreaks in other rooms within the home or business.

Toxic Black Mold Causes Neurological, Respiratory, Circulatory and Reproductive Disorders


Toxic black mold may be a common problem, but it requires immediate action. This is one of the world’s most dangerous forms of mold. It can cause neurological, respiratory, circulatory and reproductive complications, which include:

  • Brain fog;

  • Eye damage;

  • Seizure;

  • Infertility;

  • Bleeding gums;

  • And swelling of the lungs;

Are the Health Symptoms Permanent?


If the problem is left unaddressed for a prolonged period of time, certain health effects of toxic black mold may be permanent. However, most symptoms improve after removing the mold outbreak.
