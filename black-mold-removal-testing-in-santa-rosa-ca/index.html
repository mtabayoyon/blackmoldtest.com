Common Causes of Black Mold Growth


Although every strain of mold is different, most species – including black mold – thrive in warm, moist environments. However, black mold is unique in that it requires at least one week of undisturbed moisture to colonize.

If you recently suffered water damage on your property, then black mold growth is a very real possibility. Even if you removed the water, the excess moisture may be enough to set the foundation for black mold growth.

In nature, black mold grows on plant matter, but its biological properties allow it to thrive on construction materials. In a home or business, black mold can grow in basements and on top of roofs. Outbreaks can also happen behind walls due to a leaky pipe.

It does not take a substantial amount of water to cause mold growth. In fact, high humidity is enough to allow the outbreak to colonize.

Testing for Mold in House


Mold is often difficult to notice. This is especially true regarding black mold. Because it requires an undisturbed area, black mold often grows in areas that are hard to see. Unfortunately, occupants of your home or business may be exposed to deadly mycotoxins for several weeks before you suspect a black mold outbreak.

The easiest way to identify black mold is to see the growth site. This is often possible if the building suffered significant water damage. But what if the mold is behind a wall?

Mold emanates a musty odor. Many people mistake this as a “typical basement smell,” but this is a cause of concern. If you notice a musty smell in the basement or attic, schedule a black mold test immediately.

Some property owners do not suspect mold until they notice the adverse health effects. If you or a family member is having unseasonal and persistent allergy symptoms, including itchy eyes, red eyes or a swollen throat, schedule a mold inspection.

Health Issues Associated with Black Mold


Most types of mold are harmful to human health, but black mold is particularly dangerous. The mycotoxins can be absorbed through the skin or inhaled, and they can wreak havoc on several systems within the body.

Prolonged exposure can lead to coma and even death. It can also cause:


  • Nausea;

  • Vomiting;

  • Diarrhea;

  • Heart complications;

  • Shortness of breath;

  • Coughing;

  • Dizziness;

  • And cognitive disorders.
