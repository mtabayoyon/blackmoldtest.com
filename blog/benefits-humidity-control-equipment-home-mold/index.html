4 Benefits of Installing Humidity Control Equipment in Your Home – Insights about Home Mold Control

2016-12-25 · National Indoor Environmental Testing

Is a home dehumidifier worth the investment? Higher-end units can cost several hundred dollars, and if you’re like most homeowners, then you want to save money however you can.

But a home mold control unit can actually save you money in the long-run by preventing mold outbreaks, structural damage and health complications. A dehumidifier will also improve your indoor air quality and make your living space more comfortable.

Let’s take a look at the top four benefits of installing a home mold prevention system:

1. Prevents Mold Outbreaks


This is one of the primary reasons why homeowners purchase dehumidifiers. Basements, attics and other rooms with high humidity levels and poor ventilation are prone to mold growth.

According to the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency, mold can spread throughout your home via the HVAC system and cause respiratory illnesses, eye and skin irritation, allergic reactions and asthma attacks. It can also damage your drywall and foundation. If an outbreak is left unaddressed, the cost of repairs can be exorbitant.

You can prevent these issues by placing a dehumidifier in rooms that are susceptible to high humidity levels and mold growth.

2. Improves Air Quality


When you compare home dehumidifiers, you will notice that many units have optional features such as air filters. These filters capture mold spores, dust mites and other irritants to prevent them from circulating throughout your home. The result is cleaner indoor air and a lower risk of respiratory illnesses, asthma attacks and other medical complications.

3. Makes Your Living Space More Comfortable


Humidity prevents the human body from releasing heat efficiently, which leads to sweating. It’s no secret that sweating is uncomfortable and causes unattractive odors. And if you or someone in your family wants to avoid frizzy hair, you’ll face an uphill battle if your indoor air is humid.

4. Saves You Money in the Long-Run


Most homeowners choose to invest in a dehumidifier out of necessity, but they are surprised to learn that dehumidifiers actually save them money in the long-run. How? They stop mold growth, which prevents the need for structural repairs and expensive medical bills due to respiratory complications.

Also, dehumidifiers can protect your wooden furniture and floorboards. When exposed to high humidity levels, wood starts to weaken and bend. Even the most expensive dehumidifiers cost less than replacing hardwood floors and furniture.
