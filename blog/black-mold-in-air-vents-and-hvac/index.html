Black Mold in Air Vents and HVAC Systems: Risks and Solutions

2026-01-28 · National Indoor Environmental Testing

Of all the places mold can grow, your HVAC system is one of the most concerning — because it can distribute spores to every room in your home. If you've noticed mold near vents or a musty smell when the system runs, here's what you need to know.

Why HVAC Mold Is Especially Serious

Your heating and cooling system circulates air throughout the entire building. When mold grows inside ducts, the air handler, or on coils, every cycle can push spores into your living spaces. This turns a localized problem into a whole-home exposure issue.

Warning Signs of Mold in Your Vents

Watch for a musty smell that intensifies when the system turns on, visible growth around vent registers, worsening allergy or respiratory symptoms when the heat or AC runs, and excess moisture or condensation around ductwork.

Why HVAC Systems Grow Mold

Air conditioning creates condensation, and if drainage is poor or ducts are uninsulated, that moisture accumulates. Dust inside ducts provides the organic material mold needs. In the Bay Area's humid, foggy climate, these conditions are common — especially in older systems.

Why Testing Matters Before Cleaning

Before paying for duct cleaning, it's worth confirming whether mold is actually present and how far it has spread. Professional air sampling measures spore levels throughout the system and identifies the moisture source — without which, mold simply returns after cleaning.
