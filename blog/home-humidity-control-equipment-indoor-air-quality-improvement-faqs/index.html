Does Humidity Control Equipment Offer Indoor Air Quality Improvement? 3 FAQs about Home Humidity Control

2016-12-25 · National Indoor Environmental Testing

Indoor air quality improvement is one of the primary reasons why homeowners purchase dehumidifiers. A humid home is uncomfortable because the human body is less efficient at releasing heat, which leads to sweating.

But enhanced comfort is just one benefit of installing a home humidity control kit. Dehumidifiers also prevent mold outbreaks, which release spores into the air and cause respiratory illnesses, coughing, eye irritation and asthma attacks.

Is a dehumidifier a smart investment for your home? The answers to these three FAQs should help you decide:

1. How does humidity control equipment improve indoor air quality?


As previously mentioned, dehumidifiers can make your indoor air more comfortable and prevent mold growth. You can further improve your air quality by purchasing a unit with an air filter.

These filters remove dust mites, mold spores and other irritants from the air, thus protecting your family from respiratory illnesses, asthma attacks and other health complications.

2. Can humidity cause structural damage to my home? 


Yes – and the cost of repairs can be exorbitant. Basements are susceptible to high humidity levels and they have poor ventilation, which sets the foundation for mold growth. Mold can damage your drywall, and if left unaddressed, you may need to pay for an extensive restoration to resolve the problem.

Humidity can also wreak havoc on wooden furniture and floorboards. Consider the process of building an acoustic guitar: In order to get the wood to bend, it must be exposed to steam. Humidity has a similar effect on the woodwork in your home – your floorboards will bend and your wooden furniture will become weak and more susceptible to damage.

What’s more, your paint may start to peel, and water vapor could stain your walls and ceiling. A home humidity control unit can prevent these issues and save you money in the long-run.

3. Will a home humidity control system pay for itself? 


Yes. As previously mentioned, a dehumidifier will stop mold outbreaks, which can save you thousands by preventing structural damage and the cost of medical care for respiratory illnesses, allergic reactions and other complications.

If left unaddressed, high humidity can force you to replace your floorboards and furniture and repaint your home. Even the most expensive dehumidifiers cost less than these repairs.
