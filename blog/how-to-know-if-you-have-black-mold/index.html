How Do You Know If You Have Black Mold? A Homeowner's Guide

2026-01-25 · National Indoor Environmental Testing

You've spotted something dark in the corner of a room, or you've been feeling under the weather at home, and now you're wondering: is this black mold? Here's how to tell — and why guessing isn't enough.

You Can't Always Confirm Mold by Sight

Many molds look similar, and black mold (Stachybotrys) can be confused with mildew, dirt, or other dark molds. Color alone isn't reliable — only laboratory analysis can confirm the species. That's why professional testing exists: to replace guesswork with facts.

Signs That Point to Black Mold

Look for a combination of clues: dark greenish-black growth, a persistent musty smell, a history of water damage or leaks, high indoor humidity or condensation, and respiratory or allergy symptoms that improve when you leave home. The more of these you notice together, the higher the likelihood.

Where to Look

Black mold favors damp, hidden places: under sinks, behind toilets, inside walls near plumbing, in basements and crawl spaces, around windows, and inside HVAC systems. In Bay Area homes, fog-belt moisture and older construction make crawl spaces and north-facing walls common trouble spots.

What Professional Testing Involves

A certified inspection includes a visual assessment, moisture readings, and air or surface sampling analyzed by an accredited lab. The result tells you the species, the spore concentration, and — most importantly — the moisture source feeding the mold, so it can be permanently resolved.

Why Not Just Use a DIY Kit?

Home test kits often produce misleading results, can't reliably identify the source, and frequently detect mold that's naturally present in all indoor air. A professional inspection provides the accurate, actionable answer that protects your health and your property.
