Should I Purchase a Humidity Control Kit? 3 Factors to Consider before You Buy Home Mold Prevention Equipment

2016-12-31 · National Indoor Environmental Testing

Most homeowners want to save money however they can, so they gnash their teeth that the thought of dropping a few hundred dollars on a home humidity control kit. The truth, however, is that home mold prevention equipment can actually save you money by preventing mold outbreaks, protecting your wooden floors and furniture, and safeguarding your family from health issues due to poor air quality.

If you are on the fence about purchasing a home humidity control unit, consider these three factors when deciding whether to make the investment:

1. Has Your Home Experienced a Mold Outbreak in the Past? 


Mold can cause structural damage, and if left unaddressed, you might have to fork out thousands of dollars for a major restoration project. Humidity control kits prevent mold outbreaks by removing moisture from the air, thus making your living space less conducive to mold growth.

Mold releases spores into the air, which can travel throughout your home via the air ducts and cause outbreaks in other rooms. The U.S. Environmental Protection Agency warns that breathing in airborne spores can cause respiratory illnesses, asthma attacks and potentially fatal allergic reactions. Installing a home mold prevention unit will prevent these health complications and save you the cost of medical bills.

2. Do You Need a Home Mold Prevention Kit for Indoor Air Quality Improvement?


There are all sorts of optional features that come with home humidity control kits. One of the most worthwhile of these features is the air filter.

Humidity control units with air filters will remove mold spores, dust mites and other irritants from your air. This, again, will protect your family from respiratory complications and improve the quality of the air that you breathe.

3. Do You Have Hardwood Floors or Wooden Furniture?


Consider the process of making an acoustic guitar. In order to get the wood to bend, it must be exposed to steam.

Humidity has a similar effect on wooden floors and furniture. Over time, wooden floors will bend and furniture will weaken and become more vulnerable to damage. Even a top-of-the-line humidity control unit would cost less than restoring your floors and furniture.
