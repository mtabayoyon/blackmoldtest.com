7 Signs You Have Black Mold in Your Home

2026-01-18 · National Indoor Environmental Testing

Black mold often hides where you can't see it — behind walls, under floors, inside HVAC systems. Recognizing the warning signs early can save you from a bigger, costlier problem. Here are the seven most common indicators that black mold may be present in your home.

1. A Persistent Musty Smell

One of the earliest signs is a damp, earthy, musty odor that won't go away — especially in basements, bathrooms, or near HVAC vents. If a room smells musty even after cleaning, mold may be growing out of sight.

2. Visible Dark Spots or Patches

Black or greenish-black spots on walls, ceilings, grout, or window sills are the most obvious sign. Mold can look slimy when active or powdery when dried — but even dried mold still releases spores.

3. Unexplained Respiratory Symptoms

Chronic coughing, sneezing, congestion, throat irritation, or worsening asthma — especially symptoms that improve when you leave the house — can point to indoor mold exposure.

4. Water Damage or Past Leaks

Any history of flooding, roof leaks, plumbing problems, or water intrusion dramatically raises the odds of mold. Where water has been, mold often follows within 24–48 hours.

5. Peeling Paint or Bubbling Walls

Warping, bubbling, or discoloration on walls and ceilings signals trapped moisture — the exact condition mold needs to grow behind the surface.

6. Condensation Problems

Frequent condensation on windows, pipes, or walls indicates high indoor humidity, a key driver of mold growth — common in the fog-belt neighborhoods of the Bay Area.

7. Worsening Allergies at Home

If allergy symptoms flare up indoors but ease outdoors, mold spores circulating in your home's air may be the cause.
