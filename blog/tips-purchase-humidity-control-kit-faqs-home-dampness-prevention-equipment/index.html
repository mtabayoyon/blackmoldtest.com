READ THIS before You Purchase a Humidity Control Kit – 3 FAQs about Home Dampness Prevention Equipment

2016-12-31 · National Indoor Environmental Testing

If you’re like most homeowners, then you don’t want to blow money on an appliance that you don’t need. High-end humidity control kits can cost several hundred dollars, but are they really worth the investment?

Many people purchase home dampness prevention units out of necessity – for example, they may need this equipment to prevent mold outbreaks or to protect hardwood floors. However, these kits can actually save money for most homeowners in the long-run. They protect wooden furniture and floors, prevent structural damage due to mold, and safeguard your family from respiratory illnesses due to mold spores.

If you are thinking about purchasing a home humidity control kit, read on to learn three questions to ask before you make the investment:

1. How Much Does a Home Dampness Prevention Kit Cost? 


The cost of a home humidity control system will depend on several factors. Two of the most important are the capacity of your unit and its optional features. Lower-capacity dehumidifiers with simpler designs tend to be the least expensive.

2. How Can I Save Mooney When Purchasing a Home Humidity Control Kit? 


You can save money by purchasing the smallest possible dehumidifier that would still get the job done. If you only need to dehumidify a small room such as a closet or small attic, then a 30- or 50-pint kit could do the trick. However, if you want to dehumidify a large space such as your basement, then it might be wise to invest in a 70- or 100-liter unit.

You can also save money by purchasing a dehumidifier with a simpler design. There are all sorts of bells and whistles that come with dehumidifiers, and most people don’t need to buy the most expensive system. However, there are a few optional features that are particularly worthwhile including a humidistat, automatic draining, and an air filter.

You should also look for a kit with an ENERGY STAR sticker. This indicates that the dehumidifier consumes less electricity than units that are comparably effective.

3. Is a Home Dampness Prevention Unit Really Worth the Investment? 


As previously mentioned, home dehumidifiers can prevent damage to your furniture and hardwood floors. This could save you the cost and hassle of an extensive restoration.

By preventing mold growth, these systems also protect your walls and safeguard your family from respiratory illnesses, allergic reactions and asthma attacks, according to the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency. This can prevent the expense of medical bills.
