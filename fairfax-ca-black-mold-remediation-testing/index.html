Fairfax, CA Black Mold Remediation & Testing

Mold can be a business- or homeowner’s worst nightmare. In addition to causing structural damage, mold spores can become airborne and cause allergic reactions, respiratory illnesses and a multitude of other health complications. This is especially true in the case of black mold (Stachybotrys chartarum).

Black mold produces mycotoxins, which can affect the circulatory, respiratory, reproductive and neurological systems. People may consume black mold if it contaminates food, and it is even more dangerous if inhaled.

If a black mold outbreak dries, or if it is disturbed, the spores can release into the air and spread throughout a building via the HVAC system or air ducts. It is critical that you contact a mold remediation specialist before this happens.

What Causes Black Mold Growth?


Stachybotrys chartarum thrives in many climates throughout the world. It grows on plant debris and soil, as well as most construction materials – especially in warm, humid environments.

Unlike other types of mold, black mold requires an area to be moist for at least one week in order to colonize. This is common when pipes burst behind walls, basements flood or a roof leak goes unaddressed for a prolonged period of time.

How to Identify Black Mold Growth


The easiest way to identify black mold is to see the growth. Stachybotrys chartarum is a greenish-black mold that appears slimy. DO NOT touch black mold if you see an outbreak. Contact a mold removal expert as soon as possible.

Unfortunately, many families are exposed to toxic black mold for weeks or even months because the outbreak happens in a hard-to-notice area. If you, your customers or a family member shows persistent allergy symptoms, then Stachybotrys chartarum may be to blame.

Even if you can see the mold growth, this does not mean that the outbreak is confined to one area. If the mold has dried and appears grayish in color, then the spores are more likely to have released into the air. They can travel throughout a building and cause more outbreaks in warm, moist areas – such as the kitchen or bathroom. Black mold spores can also travel by attaching to people’s clothing or pets.
