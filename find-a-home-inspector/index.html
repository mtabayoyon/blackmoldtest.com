Health Risks Associated with Black Mold


Stachybotrys is one of the world’s most infamous mold strains because it is so harmful to human health. Brief exposure to black mold’s mycotoxins can cause allergy- and flu-like symptoms such as coughing, sneezing and a swollen throat. Over time, mycotoxin poisoning can lead to serious and sometimes permanent health complications – especially for people with weak immune systems such as infants and the elderly. Symptoms of mycotoxin exposure include:

  • Birth defects;

  • Internal bleeding;

  • Cognitive decline;

  • Heart inflammation;

  • Anxiety;

  • Disorientation;

  • And seizures.


Black mold can be particularly harmful to pregnant women because mycotoxin poisoning can cause birth defects. If you suspect a black mold outbreak within your home or business, do not hesitate to contact National Indoor Environmental Testing.

What Causes Black Mold Growth?


Black mold grows in warm, moist environments. In nature, it thrives on plant debris, and this adaptation allows it to wreak havoc on construction materials.

Unlike other mold strains, Stachybotrys needs at least one week to colonize. Outbreaks tend to happen in out-of-the-way places, such as basements, rooftops and behind walls.

Black mold growth is particularly common after water damage. A burst pipe behind a wall or a flooded basement can set the conditions for an outbreak. Even if you have removed the water, high humidity levels may allow the mold to colonize. This is why dehumidification is often a necessary part of the mold remediation process.

What Does Black Mold Look Like?


As its name implies, black mold is greenish-black in color. Active growth sites have slimy top layers, but if the moisture source has dissipated, then the mold may appear dry and gray. However, this does not mean that the site is harmless. Dry mold is more likely to release spores into the air, so do not disturb the outbreak.

Several mold strains look similar to black mold. The only way to know for sure if Stachybotrys has invaded your home is to schedule a professional mold inspection.
