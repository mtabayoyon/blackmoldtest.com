Causes of Toxic Black Mold Growth


Stachybotrys chartarum thrives in warm, humid areas. It grows in several countries around the globe – often on plant debris and soil. Black mold can also grow on construction materials.

Several mold species can colonize after just a few hours or days in an undisturbed, humid environment. However, toxic black mold requires at least one week to colonize. Flooded basements, burst pipes and ceiling leaks often contribute to black mold growth.

Black Mold Can Cause Permanent or Fatal Health Complications


Black mold is one of nature’s deadliest organisms. Prolonged exposure can cause reproductive problems, coma or even death. If you notice persistent allergy symptoms, then black mold may be to blame. Here are a few common health effects:

  • Fatigue;

  • Joint pain;

  • Headaches;

  • Chest pain;

  • Fever;

  • Hair loss;

  • And diarrhea.


These symptoms will gradually worsen over time. The elderly and infants are at a particularly high risk of adverse reactions due to their weak immune systems. Testing for Mold in House


Many business- and homeowners mistakenly assume that mold outbreaks are easy to identify. They put off the mold inspection until they see the growth or notice the telltale musty smell. However, black mold can be particularly hard to spot. It only grows in undisturbed areas.

If you do notice a black mold outbreak, keep your distance. Touching it or inhaling the spores can cause an immediate allergic reaction. Call a mold removal specialist as soon as possible.

Some people only learn they have a mold outbreak after suffering persistent health ailments. Itchy eyes, stuffy nose, skin irritation, coughing and other allergy-like symptoms can indicate black mold growth. If you do not normally have allergies, or if these symptoms appear out of season, contact a mold remediation expert immediately.

Even if you can see the mold, you should never attempt to clean it without professional help. This may not be the only outbreak – especially if the mold has dried and released spores into the air. A professional mold inspector can use a moisture meter to identify areas where mold is likely to grow within your building.
