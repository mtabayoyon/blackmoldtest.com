Home Test for Black Mold


Black mold gets its name from its blackish-green color. Active sites have a slimy layer on top, but if the source of the moisture has dissipated, then the site may appear dry and gray. This, however, does not mean that the mold is dead. In fact, dry mold sites are more likely to release spores.

If you see a black mold growth site, stay away and get everyone out of the building immediately. Schedule a mold inspection as soon as possible.

Unfortunately, black mold usually grows in areas that are difficult to see. These include basements, behind walls and on rooftops. Black mold detection is particularly common after water damage. If your basement was flooded, or if you notice a water leak behind a wall, then mold growth should be your main concern.

Mold also emanates a characteristic musty smell. Many people write off this scent as a typical basement odor, but you should be cautious if you notice an abnormal, unpleasant smell in your basement.

Health Risks Associated with Black Mold


Black mold often makes media headlines because it is so dangerous to human health. Mycotoxins are particularly hazardous for people with weak immune systems, such as senior citizens and young children.

There are dozens of mycotoxins, but trichothecene mycotoxins are among the most dangerous. In fact, they have been used in biological weapons. They are particularly difficult to kill. Not all black mold sites release trichothecene mycotoxins, but you should always expect the worst.

Mycotoxins can affect the cardiovascular, respiratory, reproductive and neurological systems. The signs of brief exposure are often similar to the flu: Coughing, sneezing, runny nose and itchy eyes are common. However, prolonged exposure can cause:


  • Internal bleeding;

  • Disorientation;

  • Hallucinations;

  • Blood clot disorders;

  • Heart inflammation;

  • Nausea;

  • Fever;

  • Diarrhea;

  • And vomiting.


This is not a complete list of symptoms. Prolonged exposure can cause serious cognitive impairment – even leading to coma or death. Mycotoxin poisoning can also cause infertility, miscarriage, fetal development problems and other reproductive conditions.
