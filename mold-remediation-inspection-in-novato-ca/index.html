Mold Remediation & Inspection in Novato, CA

Mold growth may be a common nuisance, but it can be a business- or homeowners worst nightmare. The property destruction is just the beginning. Black mold (Stachybotrys), in particular, can cause severe and potentially fatal health complications.

The secret behind black mold’s fatal punch is its mycotoxins. These can spread throughout a building via the HVAC system. Mycotoxins can enter the body through the skin or inhalation.

Initial exposure to mycotoxins can cause coughing, dizziness and red eyes, but over time, they can affect a person’s neurological, cardiovascular, respiratory and reproductive systems. Infants and the elderly are at a particularly high risk of mycotoxin poisoning, but healthy adults can also suffer severe health complications.

Black Mold: A Serious Health Threat


Many home- and business owners associate mold growth with property damage. Although black mold can destroy construction materials, its health effects should be your top concern.

The mycotoxins within black mold can affect several systems within the body. If you notice unseasonal allergy symptoms that seem to persist, schedule a black mold test immediately.

The symptoms will worsen over time. If left unaddressed, the effects of mycotoxin poisoning may be permanent. In most cases, though, the symptoms dissipate after removing the growth site.

Prolonged exposure to black mold may cause:


  • Vomiting;

  • Nausea;

  • Muscle weakness;

  • Diarrhea;

  • Chest pain;

  • Abdominal discomfort;

  • Blurred vision;

  • Infertility;

  • Miscarriage;

  • Joint discomfort;

  • And nose bleeds.


Black mold is especially dangerous for young children and senior citizens. It can also cause fetal complications for pregnant women.

What Causes Black Mold Growth?


Like most mold species, black mold requires a moist, warm area to grow. However, Stachybotrys needs at least one week to colonize while other species can colonize within a few hours or days.

As a result, black mold tends to grow in areas that are difficult to see. Outbreaks are common behind walls due to leaky pipes, basements and on top of roofs.

If your building suffers water damage, mold growth should be your top concern. Even if you remove most of the moisture, the remaining humidity can set the foundation for an outbreak.

Many property owners do not suspect a black mold growth until somebody complains about persistent allergies. This is a telltale sign. You should also be cautious if you notice a musty smell.

In the outdoors, black mold thrives on plant matter. Its biological adaptation allows it to grow on construction materials, which is why it can be so destructive to businesses and homes.
