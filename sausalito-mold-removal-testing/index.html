What Causes Black Mold Outbreaks?


Like most other mold strains, Stachybotrys thrives in warm environments with plenty of moisture. Black mold needs at least one week to colonize, which is much longer than other mold species.

Growth sites tend to form in undisturbed areas, such as rooftops, basements and behind walls. Outbreaks often follow water damage, but black mold doesn’t need a significant amount of moisture to grow. Sometimes all it takes is a high humidity level to set the foundation for an outbreak.

In nature, black mold grows on plant matter and in the soil. This biological adaptation allows it to thrive on construction materials, such as drywall and wood. As a result, black mold is a common nuisance in homes and businesses.

If you suspect a mold outbreak in your home, do not search for active sites. Inhaling mycotoxins is more dangerous than actually touching them, so you can still suffer adverse health effects without physical contact.

Black mold typically appears greenish-black in color, hence its name. However, dried sites may look grayish if the source of moisture has dissipated. This does not mean that the site is harmless. In fact, dried black mold is more likely to release spores.

Stachybotrys spores can spread throughout abuilding via the HVAC system.  Spores can land in other rooms, and if the conditions are ideal, another outbreak can form.

Mycotoxins in Black Mold Can Be Deadly


The structural damage that black mold causes should be the least of your concerns. Building occupants can suffer severe – and sometimes permanent – health effects due to prolonged exposure to mycotoxins. Symptoms include:

  • Coughing;

  • Sneezing;

  • Internal bleeding;

  • Heart palpitations;

  • Nausea;

  • Vomiting blood;

  • And blurry vision.


Mycotoxins can also cause cognitive decline. Senior citizens and infants are especially susceptible to the adverse health effects due to their weak immune systems. Pregnant women, in particular, must avoid mycotoxin poisoning because it can cause birth defects.

There are several types of mycotoxins. Some black mold sites produce trichothecene mycotoxins, which are so deadly that they have been used in biochemical warfare. This is why you should not hesitate to contact a mold removal expert as soon as you suspect an outbreak.
